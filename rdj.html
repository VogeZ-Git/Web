Rule #1

Your Network

"Make friends with people who want the best for you. Surround yourself with supportive and positive people who encourage your growth and well-being."
Why it matters
Choosing your social circle wisely is essential—these people will uplift you, provide positive influence, and inspire you. They’ll offer encouragement and support your growth. If you observe successful individuals, you'll notice that they often surround themselves with other successful people.
Rule #2

Two Systems of Thought

"Be aware of the two systems of thought and use System 2 for all investing decisions."
The Theory
Popularized by Daniel Kahneman, this theory divides our thinking into two distinct processes:

System 1: Fast, automatic, and subconscious. It relies on intuition and emotion for everyday tasks like recognizing faces or reacting to immediate danger.

System 2: Slow, deliberate, and logical. This system is engaged for complex problem-solving and careful analysis. It helps us avoid snap judgments influenced by biases.
Conclusion
In the context of wealth, System 1 is often your enemy. Use the slower, deliberate reasoning of System 2 to filter out emotional noise and make important financial choices.
Rule #3

Patience

"The market is a weapon that transfers money from the 'impatient' to the 'patient.' Getting rich slowly is the best way forward; speed kills."
The Three Pillars of Patience
  • Long-Term Perspective: Successful investors hold through fluctuations, understanding that short-term volatility is a normal part of the market cycle.
  • Emotional Discipline: Staying calm during uncertainty avoids hasty decisions driven by fear or greed. If you've been through enough storms, you don't sweat the noise.
  • Delayed Gratification: While human nature seeks "Get Rich Quick" schemes, true wealth comes from allowing investments to mature over time.
Rule #4

Inverted Thinking

"Invert, always invert. Instead of looking for how to win, look for all the ways you could lose—then avoid them."
The Munger Method
Inspired by Charlie Munger, this rule is about stepping into the "other shoes" and looking at the downside first. Invert your logic to protect your capital.

  • Avoid Loss vs. Seek Gains: Ask "What could cause me to lose money on this?" rather than just "How much will I make?"
  • Spotting the 'Killer': By looking for what could destroy a thesis (like an "ETH Killer"), you identify superior plays. This is how I moved from ETH to SOL—the data showed a better path.
  • Crush Confirmation Bias: Don't seek info that supports your view. Actively seek data that contradicts your thesis.
IA Insight: Emotions are powerful, but the Data Don't Lie. Build models to stay objective.
Rule #5

Only Invest in What You Know

"Only invest in businesses you fully comprehend. Risk comes from not knowing what you're doing."
Stay in Your Circle of Competence
Straying from core expertise—like technology, finance, and crypto—often leads to significant losses. Maintaining focus on what you understand is the ultimate risk mitigator.

  • Reduced Uncertainty: Understanding a business allows for accurate evaluation of financial health and competitive positioning. For example, knowing that most Bitcoin miners are low-quality helps avoid bad bets.
  • Spotting Early Opportunities: Deep industry knowledge lets you recognize undervalued companies or emerging trends before the broader market. Conversely, acknowledging what you don't know (like Biotech) is just as important.
  • Resilience Against FUD: When you have spent thousands of hours researching a play like Tesla or Solana, you have the confidence to buy when others panic or when "bears and haters" claim the asset is dead.
Rule #6

Make a Plan, Stick to it!

"Stick to your investment strategy even during volatile market conditions. Success starts with a plan, and step two is building toward it without getting shaken out."
Top Three Takeaways
Consistency and discipline are the primary components of long-term financial success. A well-thought-out plan acts as your anchor when the market becomes irrational.

  • Avoid Emotional Reactions: Getting shaken out at market bottoms can be catastrophic. Whether playing complex option strategies on MicroStrategy or holding spot, sticking to the plan during near-liquidation events is what leads to big payoffs.
  • Focus on Long-Term Goals: Align your plan with long-term objectives. High-conviction holdings like Tesla and Bitcoin are easier to hold through volatility when you know they are positioned for massive long-term growth.
  • Consistency Over Time: Set specific targets—like a fixed number of BTC or TSLA shares—and stack toward them. Benchmark these against price target models and maintain strict control over your allocations.
Rule #7

Allocations Matter

"Allocations are the cornerstone of a successful investment strategy. Don't be tempted to get rich quick—get rich slowly and safely by always managing your risk."
Risk Management & Guardrails
Proper allocation is the only way to balance risk and reward while ensuring your portfolio remains resilient against market volatility. Disregarding these rules is how accounts get blown up.

  • Risk Categorization: Distinguish between core long-term holdings like Bitcoin or Tesla and higher-risk plays like miners, perps, or leveraged positions. Carefully distributing capital across these profiles limits exposure to catastrophic loss.
  • Strict Personal Rules: Set "never" benchmarks to resist temptation: Never loan out more than 10% of your Bitcoin; never put more than 5% in miners; never exceed 1% in a perp position. Don't pick up pennies in front of a steamroller.
  • Accountability: Regularly tracking and sharing allocations provides a reality check on how assets are managed in response to actual market conditions versus emotional impulses.
IA Insight: Greek Kills. Stick to your allocation guidelines to protect your financial well-being from the lure of overexposure.
Rule #8

Know Poker

"Poker is the ultimate training ground for investing. It teaches you to make positive expected value decisions in uncertain environments with imperfect information."
How Poker Enhances Investment Skills
The skills required to win at the poker table translate directly to winning in the markets. It’s about more than just cards; it’s about psychology, math, and discipline.

  • Calculated Risk & Probabilities: Like assessing a hand, investing requires constant evaluation of odds and potential scenarios. This probability-based thinking is the engine behind high-level valuation models.
  • Reading the Bluff: Analyzing opponents helps in identifying the agendas of Wall Street analysts. Are they full of it, or are they faking it? Poker intuition helps you spot the bluff.
  • Emotional Control & "Tilt": Avoiding "tilt"—emotional reactions to a bad beat—is critical. Successful players stay calm under pressure, even when significant money is at stake.
  • Adapting & Sizing: You must update your strategy as new "cards" (market data) are revealed. Know when to hold 'em and when to fold 'em, while ensuring your bet sizing (allocations) keeps you in the game.
IA Insight: Poker is a zero-sum game. The question is always: are you smarter than the person on the other side of the trade? If not, you will lose.
Rule #9

Buy When Fear is High

"Buy when there's blood in the streets. My philosophy is to run into the burning building when everyone else is fleeing."
The Contrarian Advantage
Success in investing often requires blazing your own trail and buying assets when market sentiment is extremely negative and fear has reached its peak.

  • Potential for High Returns: Market turmoil often pushes prices into severe oversold conditions. Buying at these lows can lead to substantial gains during the eventual recovery.
  • Exploiting Indiscriminate Selling: Widespread fear causes even fundamentally sound investments to be sold off. This creates massive opportunities for those willing to go against the crowd.
  • Reduced Competition: During a panic, there are very few buyers in the market. This lack of a "bid" crashes prices, which is why maintaining cash is vital to exploit these moments of capitulation.
Rule #10

Being Grateful

"Being grateful rewires the brain. The minute you stop worrying about 'not enough' and focus on 'plenty' is the minute you become better at managing money."
How Gratitude Sharpens the Investing Mind
Focusing on thankfulness releases serotonin and dopamine, reducing stress and shifting your perspective from scarcity to abundance.

  • Making Better Choices: Gratitude naturally increases patience—the most critical asset in investing. It helps you transition to a mindset where you make smarter, less desperate financial decisions.
  • Emotional Balance: Staying thankful helps you handle market ups and downs without overreacting. It is the secret to staying positive and calm even at the bottom of a brutal bear market.
  • Big Picture Focus: Instead of obsessing over what is going wrong, gratitude trains you to see what is going well and keep your eyes fixed on the long game.
IA Practice: Write down or verbally share what you are thankful for every day—even small things like the weather or your morning tea. It changes how your brain processes the world.
Rule #11

Remain Rational w Data

"I refuse to make any investment decisions without the backing of a robust data model. Get your head in the spreadsheet—the data don't lie."
The Power of Quant
Embracing a model-driven approach reduces the impact of emotional biases and cognitive errors. Using quantitative analysis ensures your strategy is rational, consistent, and data-centric.

  • Objective Risk Assessment: Models quantify risk factors more accurately than subjective judgment. By analyzing historical data and market trends, you can make decisions based on statistical probabilities rather than fear or greed.
  • Performance Forecasting: Predictive models, like those for Tesla and Bitcoin, help set realistic expectations and identify opportunities overlooked by emotional reasoning. They prevent impulsive reactions to optimistic or pessimistic outlooks.
  • Systematic Execution: Models ensure you stick to predetermined criteria and avoid "hot tips." Whether it's the Profit Taking Model or the SCP Profiler, objective metrics beat gut feelings every time.
IA Lesson: After losing money on a "sure thing" tip in 1993, I became vehemently opposed to external hype. Always do your own research and build your own models.
Rule #12

Don’t Lose Money

"Rule No. 1: Never lose money. Rule No. 2: Never forget rule No. 1. Protect your capital at all costs—do not play with fire."
Capital Preservation & Wealth Building
Building wealth is as much about what you keep as what you make. Consistency beats "chasing rabbits" or jumping into every asset that pops 10% in a day.

  • Power of Compounding: Avoiding significant losses allows your capital to compound uninterrupted. Even small losses have a massive negative impact on long-term returns; keeping your capital intact ensures more money is working for you every year.
  • Avoiding Permanent Loss: Market fluctuations are normal, but permanent loss of capital (investing in non-pristine assets that never recover) impairs your ability to grow. Focus on "pristine assets" like BTC and TSLA that have the fundamentals to rise again.
  • Emotional Stability: Avoiding "blow-ups" prevents panic. You don't need 100x moonshots to achieve your dreams; a series of solid 10x returns from researched, high-conviction plays is enough to reach your goals without the stress of ruin.
Rule #13

Undervalued Assets

"Look beyond traditional metrics to identify companies poised for transformative growth. The key is to stack as much as you can when the market underappreciates them."
Finding and Stacking Value
Combining value investing principles with disruptive potential allows you to position a portfolio for massive long-term growth. You must have the courage to acquire the top 1% of assets when they are discounted.

  • Identify Undervalued Disruptors: Focus on companies with strong fundamentals and innovative business models that the market doesn't yet understand. Look for plays like MSTR, TSLA, CLSK, or SOL.

  • Look Beyond the Ratios: Don't just look at financial ratios. Evaluate a company’s ability to innovate and disrupt—whether it’s taking market share from ETH, disrupting legacy industries, or utilizing the "Infinite Money Glitch."
Rule #14

Don't Trade When Tired or Sick

"Trading while exhausted or unwell impairs your decision-making and leads to costly mistakes. If your brain isn't at 100%, step away from the terminal."
The Danger of Impaired Cognitive Function
When your body is compromised, so is your capital. Physical and mental exhaustion lead to slower reaction times, difficulty analyzing charts, and a reduced ability to accurately assess risk.

  • Loss of Discipline: Fatigue causes instability, making you more likely to deviate from your established trading plan and neglect your usual rhythm.
  • Case in Point: Even with an amazing chart setup for SOL, being tired can lead to premature exits. While de-risking is part of the strategy, doing so purely because of mental exhaustion can cost you significant gains.
  • Careless Errors: Without focus, you miss the nuance in the data. Never let a temporary illness or a lack of sleep turn into a permanent loss of wealth.
Rule #15

Desperation Means Danger!

"In turbulent markets, calm is your greatest asset. Desperation breeds costly mistakes; wealth accumulates gradually, not overnight."
The Stoic Investor's Advantage
Embrace stoicism in your investing approach. While the crowd acts on emotion and panic, the professional acts on reason and timing.

  • Avoid the YOLO Trap: Market seasonality is key. If you turn up late to the party, do not impulsively YOLO in. Patience always outperforms desperate action.
  • Strategic Cash Reserves: Maintaining a cash position (like my current 9%) isn't a failure—it's readiness. There is no rush to "blow" your cash; wait for the right entry, especially when carrying leverage elsewhere.
  • Reason Over Emotion: Emotional traders make the most mistakes in volatile times. Staying composed allows you to maintain your discipline and protect your long-term wealth accumulation.
IA Insight: Desperation is a signal of weakness. Let others panic while you remain the stoic observer.
Rule #16

Sunk Cost Fallacy

"Ignore what you've already spent. The market doesn't care what price you bought at—focus only on the present information and future potential."
The Danger of Holding On
The sunk cost fallacy leads to irrational decisions based on past effort or money that cannot be recovered. Refusing to sell a loser just to avoid "realizing a loss" is a trap that prevents you from winning.

  • The Double Loss: When you hold a failing asset, you don't just lose money; you lose the opportunity cost of not being on a "faster horse." You are losing twice by not moving capital to high-potential plays.
  • Emotional Detachment: Never get married to an asset. Breakeven is not a strategy—many assets will never return to their all-time highs. Objective analysis must always override the hope of "getting back to even."
  • Cleaning Shop: Regularly review positions based on current conditions, not past entries. Implement strict exit strategies for both the upside and downside before you ever enter a trade.
IA Insight: To win, you must be rational. If you don't respect the data over your emotions, the market will rip your face off.
Rule #17

Disruptive Supercycles

"Identify early and invest hard in major technological shifts. The disruption happening now with AI and Crypto is on a level we have never seen before."
Riding the Waves of Innovation
Technological supercycles are transformative waves that reshape industries and create enormous economic value. To win, you must spot them in their nascent stages and back the winners aggressively.

  • Spot Emerging Cycles: Develop a keen eye for radical innovations that have the potential to disrupt existing markets. History shows that those who recognized Storage, Search, and eCommerce early built massive wealth.
  • Invest Hard in Winners: Once a supercycle is identified, commit substantially. This requires taking calculated risks on technologies that may seem speculative to the mainstream—like buying Tesla at $15 when the world predicted bankruptcy.
  • Ecosystem Evolution: Supercycles involve entire ecosystems. Stay alert and be ready to adapt; a leader in the early stage might be overtaken by a superior disruptor later, such as the shift from ETH to the "ETH Killer" SOL.
IA Insight: These cycles can take years to fully materialize. Combine continuous learning with extreme patience to weather the volatility.
Rule #18

Timeframes

"Process is everything. Discover your unique rhythm and stick to it religiously—the further you move toward long-term perspectives, the higher your odds of success."
Finding the Golden Rhythm
After three and a half decades of testing every time series, the data shows that timeframe selection is paramount. Success is found by matching your strategy to a timeframe that maximizes probability while minimizing emotional noise.

  • The Swing Trader Advantage: The 4-hour timeframe is the "golden" window. It allows for capturing large trends and movements without the stress of scalping, helping maintain a high win ratio (80%) by staying on the right side of the trend.
  • The Danger of Day Trading: Approximately 95% of day traders lose money within a year. Short-term charts (5-minute) often promote over-trading and churn, leading to unnecessary losses and high exit rates from the market.
  • Long-Term Investing: Moving to daily charts and multi-year holds (like BTC and TSLA since 2017) requires the most patience but offers the broadest perspective and most reliable value growth.
IA Insight: Consistency separates successful traders from the rest. Identify your winning formula—whether it's the 4-hour swing or the long-term hold—and apply your process consistently.
Rule #19

Be Confident - Use Your Brain

"Don't be intimidated by Wall Street. You have an edge: the ability to spot opportunities in your everyday life that the 'experts' miss."
The Individual Investor's Edge
The best investment opportunities often hide in plain sight, but you have to experience them to understand their true value. Trust your own intelligence and observations over the noise of fund managers.

  • Real-World Experience: Experiencing a product or service first-hand provides deeper conviction than any spreadsheet. Buying a Model X in 2017 was the catalyst that led to "nibbling" on TSLA and eventually buying heavy LEAPS in 2019 when the price was just $17.50.
  • Confidence is Key: Success in the market requires the confidence to act on your own research. If you wait for Wall Street to validate your thesis, the "easy money" has already been made.
  • Spotting Disruption Early: Whether it's Tesla, MicroStrategy, or others, individual investors can identify special companies long before institutional money catches up. Don't underestimate your ability to see what's coming next.
IA Insight: The minute you realize you don't need a suit to be right is the minute you start winning. Use your brain—don't be intimidated.
Rule #20

Reverse Mistakes Fast

"Investing is a blood sport—bury your pride. If you realize you've made a mistake, admit it quickly and take action to reverse course immediately."
The Cost of Stubbornness
Psychological barriers often prevent investors from admitting they are wrong. Whether you bought a poor-quality asset or sold a winner too early, the ability to act decisively and swallow your pride is what saves your portfolio.

  • Recognize and Act: Be vigilant and honest about performance. Reversing a position at the right moment can save a fortune—like covering an MSTR hedge before a massive move, saving the equivalent of 10 Cybertrucks in a single day.
  • Overcome Rationalization: Human nature is to "hope" things turn around or to rationalize poor decisions (like holding ADA). Overcome this tendency; once a mistake is identified, exit or reverse promptly.
  • Case in Point: Analysts who can't swallow their pride often miss the biggest gains. Selling the bottom of a high-conviction asset like Tesla and refusing to get back in can lead to missing out on nearly 90% in gains.
IA Insight: Be humble. Learn from the experience so you don't repeat it. If you are wrong, be wrong fast.
Rule #21

Sit On Your Hands

"Resist the urge to act. Sometimes doing nothing is the most productive thing you can do for your portfolio—trust your research, trust the data, and ignore the media."
The Power of Restraint
When markets are turbulent or faced with major geopolitical events, the natural inclination is to "do something." This rule emphasizes that patience and restraint often lead to better long-term outcomes than reactive trading.

  • Establish a Confidence Threshold: Don't make significant changes unless you hit a high degree of certainty (e.g., an 80% chance of a favorable outcome). If your long-term thesis is intact, stay the course even when it is painful.
  • Tax and Friction Efficiency: Sitting on your hands minimizes transaction costs and avoids triggering massive capital gains taxes. Sometimes the "tax cost" of selling is the best deterrent against emotional decision-making.
  • Emotional Detachment: Volatility triggers fear and anxiety. By consciously deciding to "sit on your hands," you bypass these emotions and remain focused on your long-term goals rather than short-term noise.
IA Insight: Trust your gut and your models, not the media. Accepting that market uncertainty is normal leads to a lower-stress, more successful investing life.
Rule #22

Hesitation Kills

"Overcome analysis paralysis and act decisively on well-researched opportunities. Don't squabble over pennies when you're looking at an asset headed for the moon."
The Cost of Doing Nothing
Hesitation, often born from fear of volatility or over-analysis, is one of the most expensive mistakes an investor can make. Waiting for the "perfect" entry often results in missing the move entirely or entering at significantly higher prices.

  • Avoid the Entry Trap: Don't let a $20 difference on a $100 stock stop you when your model shows it going to $2,000. Real-world cases, like missing Bitcoin at $3,000 or Tesla at $110, show that the cost of "waiting for a reversal" is often a permanent loss of massive gains.
  • Psychological Barriers: Anxiety about market timing often leads to decision paralysis. This keeps investors sitting on cash for years while the market appreciates, resulting in minimal position sizes and missed compounding.
  • Strategies for Action: If you can't "bite the bullet," use Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) or better yet, our DCAS model to automate your entries. Start small to build comfort, but the key is to start.
IA Insight: Make a plan and stick to it. Whether it's becoming a Wholecoiner or reaching your retirement goal, the biggest risk isn't volatility—it's being left behind.
Rule #23

Don’t Compare Yourself to Others

"Resist the temptation to switch and chase. Everyone’s journey is different—focus on your own strategy and celebrate the wins of others without envy."
Defeating Toxic Tribalism
The investment space, particularly crypto, is often filled with stories of overnight gains that trigger inadequacy and FOMO. Comparing your progress to others leads to poor decisions driven by greed rather than sound logic.

  • Avoid the Chasing Trap: Every asset has its day in the sun. If you see another coin pumping while yours is flat, don't abandon your well-researched plan just to chase someone else's green candle. Stick to your big bets and ride them out.
  • Rise Above the Tribes: Resentment between "tribes"—whether it's BTC Maxis vs. Altcoiners or Gold Bugs vs. Tech Investors—is a distraction. A healthy investor celebrates the success of the broader market while maintaining their own disciplined allocations.
  • Focus on Individual Growth: Your financial goals and risk tolerance are unique to you. Letting your winners ride based on your targets is far more profitable than trying to mirror the "overnight" success of strangers on the internet.
IA Insight: Tribalism is noise. Diversity among high-conviction assets allows you to participate in the best of all worlds without getting bogged down in the "Us vs. Them" mentality.

Seeing the Future Early
Extraordinary wealth comes from seeing structural shifts before the mainstream. This requires an immense time commitment to analysis and the courage to act while the world is still doubting.

IA Insight: If you wait for the consensus to agree with you, the opportunity is gone. Do the work, find the disruptor, and bet big.
Rule #25

READ

"Reading is the compound interest of the mind. The more you read, the more your knowledge builds, creating a deeper understanding that others will simply miss."
Knowledge as a Competitive Edge
Successful investing isn't just about watching tickers; it's about building a massive mental library. Reading newspapers, financial reports, 10-Ks, and biographies allows you to see patterns and business models before the market prices them in.

  • Develop Unique Perspectives: By consuming diverse materials—from macroeconomics and finance to tech whitepapers—you learn why businesses succeed or fail. This "compound knowledge" helps you spot the next big disruptor.
  • Strengthen Strategic Connections: Being well-read enriches your conversations with other high-level investors. It allows you to cross-reference data points and gain viewpoints that challenge your own biases.
  • The Balanced Approach: Don't just be a bookworm. Combine your reading with real-world interactions and active exploration. Use what you read to inform your actions—reading without execution is just entertainment.
IA Insight: Like Buffett, spend hours a day reading. It is the only way to become "wiser" and develop the temperament to grab the correct ideas when they appear.
Rule #26

Time and Place

"Investing is like changing seasons. Successful investors recognize the current market 'season' and adapt their strategies to match the climate."
Strategic Adaptability
One size does not fit all. To maximize returns and minimize drawdown, you must pivot your tactics based on whether the market is at a bottom, chopping sideways, or in a full-blown bull run.

  • Bear Market Bottom: This is the time to be aggressive. Use synthetic longs (selling puts to buy 18-month+ calls) to capture the eventual rebound with maximum leverage.
  • Sideways Chop: When the market is range-riding, implement covered calls. Selling calls against stagnant positions like MSTR or CLSK generates income while you wait for the next breakout.
  • Explosive Bull Market: Don't get cute and don't go short. Hold your winners tight and amplify your yield through staking (e.g., SOL) rather than over-trading.
  • Volatile Periods: Utilize tax-free accounts for scalping or pair trading (e.g., MSTR vs. TSLA). This captures quick swings without losing 20-30% of your gains to the taxman.
  • Profit-Taking Phase: As you hit your goals, transition to hedging or rotate into cash and "laggard" assets with massive remaining upside.
IA Insight: Your goals are the compass, but the market season determines the gear you wear. Never try to fight the weather—adjust to it.
Rule #27

Risk Mgt w Leverage

"Leverage is a double-edged sword. You can win 15 times in a row and lose it all on the 16th if you don't respect the math. Never allow a liquidation event."
Managing the Juggernaut
Trading with leverage requires extreme discipline and a "worst-case scenario" mindset. When the geopolitical landscape shifts, your risk management rules must be ironclad to protect your capital.

  • Correcting the Equity-to-Cash Imbalance: As your portfolio value explodes, your cash-to-equity ratio naturally falls (e.g., $700 cash in a $10k account is 7%, but in a $20k account, it’s only 3.5%). This erosion of your liquidity base makes you vulnerable. Fortification means replenishing these reserves to restore your strategic balance.
  • Case Study: Tesla Leverage: Using MSTR gains to sell Tesla puts and buy LEAPS was a bold offensive strike. Now that the position has delivered, "letting it cook" means resisting the urge to roll gains into fresh risk. Shift from expansion to endurance.
  • Harvesting for the Next Swan: Realized gains should be redirected into cash or short-term, low-risk options. By reloading now, you position yourself as a "predator" ready to strike when the next gray or black swan event inevitably creates a bargain.
IA Insight: If your cash position hasn't grown at the same rate as your portfolio, you are technically becoming more fragile as you get richer. Don't let your success make you vulnerable—hoard your victory.
Rule #88

Nothing More Painful Than Top Buying

"Never chase euphoria. Buy fear, not peaks. Buying at market tops is the fastest way to lock in future regret and psychological torment."
The Peril of Euphoric Accumulation
Wealth is built by accumulating when sentiment is bleak and prices are in the "bargain basement." Conversely, the most destructive financial and emotional agony comes from buying into a rally when "everyone is winning." At market tops, risk is at its absolute highest because valuations have detached from reality. If you don't have the discipline to stop buying, you will end up holding expensive assets through a multi-year drawdown.

  • The BitMine Lesson: BitMine Immersion Technologies (BMNR) serves as a stark 2025 warning. Led by Tom Lee, they pivoted to an Ethereum treasury strategy, aggressively accumulating millions of ETH. However, much of this buying happened at elevated levels, leading to billions in unrealized losses as the market cooled.
  • Sweating the Cost Basis: When your cost basis is significantly higher than the current price (e.g., Tom Lee’s ETH basis near $4,100 while the market sits below $3,900), you aren't just losing money—you are losing "Opportunity Cost." That capital is trapped while other assets (like SOL or TSLA) are making moves.
  • Patience Over Excitement: Tops look safe because the momentum feels unstoppable. But real safety is found in the "Extreme Fear" zone of the Fear & Greed Index. Stopping your purchases early protects your capital and, more importantly, your peace of mind.
  • Reputational Risk: For institutional players, top-buying can destroy a legacy. If ETH fails to reclaim those euphoric levels, the narrative shifts from "visionary" to "bag holder" in the dustbin of market history.
IA Insight: If you are tempted to "FOMO" into a green candle, look at the 200-day moving average. If the price is extended far above it, you aren't investing; you're donating your capital to the people who bought the bottom.

Rule #92

The "Coachella Rental"

"Selling a Covered Call isn't selling your asset—it’s renting out a room in your house during a peak festival. You keep the property, but you charge the guest a premium for the stay."
Renting Your Stock for Passive Yield
Many investors confuse a hedge with an exit. Selling an Out-of-the-Money (OTM) Covered Call is a strategic income play, not a lack of conviction. Just as a prime property near a festival site commands massive rent during Coachella, your high-volatility assets (like Tesla) command massive "rent" (premiums) during mean reversion spikes.

  • The Festival Rate (Volatility Spikes): You don't rent your house cheap on a Tuesday. You wait for the "Festival"—the large upside spikes where call premiums are bloated. By selling when volatility is high, you capture the maximum "stay rate" from the option buyer.
  • The "Out-of-the-Money" Buffer: This is your safety zone. By setting a strike price 10-15% above the current market, the stock has to make a massive move before you even consider "selling the house." You participate in the growth up to that strike while pocketing the cash today.
  • Profiting from the Clock (Theta Decay): In the options world, time is a physical asset. A 39-day call only has 26 trading days of life. As the "weekend" expires, the value of the option decays toward zero. This **Theta Decay** is the rent you keep regardless of price movement, provided the stock stays below your strike.
  • Yield Without Exit: Imagine $100K in covered calls generating ~$3,800 in "rent" per trading day. This cash flow can be used to pay taxes, fund new positions, or simply rebuild your cash ammunition (Rule #87) without ever touching your core long-term position.
IA Insight: Selling covered calls is a "singles and doubles" strategy. It won't hit the home run, but it builds the bankroll that keeps you in the game during the long winters. Never sell your house; just charge the visitors a fortune to look at the view.
Rule #93

Rule #94

"Margin Magic"

"Use the equity in deep-in-the-money LEAPS to buy stock for less cash than the stock’s actual loan value. It’s an infinite money glitch that converts leveraged gains into permanent borrowing power."
The Three-Step Formula for Leveraged Compounding
Margin Magic is an advanced strategy for accelerating wealth by transitioning from high-leverage options to high-equity stock positions with zero net cash outlay. By exercising deep-in-the-money (ITM) LEAPS and using the resulting stock as collateral, you effectively get paid in "borrowing power" to own the asset.

  • 1. The Synthetic Start: Enter a "Synthetic Long" (buy a LEAP call, sell a put) at a low net cost. This provides 100% delta exposure (full upside/downside) for a fraction of the capital of buying shares. Over 2 years, as the stock moves from $150 to $480, your $140 strike call gains massive intrinsic value.
  • 2. The Margin Exercise: Instead of selling the call for a taxable gain, you exercise it. A standard 50% margin account allows you to cover the $140 strike price with only $70 in equity.
  • 3. The "Glitch" Unlock: Because the stock is now worth $480, its "Loan Value" (50% of market price) is $240. Since the strike you paid ($140) is significantly less than the loan value you just created ($240), the broker effectively lends you the entire purchase price. You now own the shares with a $0 cash outlay and have $100 in leftover "free" buying power.
  • The Infinite Loop: You now have a "Mattress Position" of shares. You can sell covered calls (Rule #92) against them to pay off the margin interest, or use the newly created buying power to enter your next asymmetric bet.
IA Warning: Margin is a double-edged sword. If the stock tanks below your "break-even" (strike + interest), you face a margin call. I only use 10-20% of my margin capacity, keeping the rest for "running into burning buildings" during black swans.
Rule #95

The "Turbo Booster"

"Transform a low-cost, long-term holding into a cash-flow engine by systematically selling covered calls. This is the mechanism that funds a lifetime of freedom from a single high-conviction bet."
Yield on Cost: The 1,440% Return
The "Turbo Booster" is the ultimate evolution of the **Coachella Rental (Rule #92)**. It applies to "Mattress Positions" where you have a significant number of shares at an extremely low cost basis. By selling calls on massive mean reversion spikes, you aren't just earning "extra money"—you are generating astronomical returns relative to your original investment.

  • The "Free House" Setup: This works best on volatile, high-growth assets (like TSLA) where you entered early (e.g., $12.50/share) or used **Margin Magic (Rule #94)** to acquire shares with $0 cash outlay. When your basis is near zero, every dollar of premium is pure "Turbo" for your portfolio.
  • Renting the Upside: You don't hope for a rally; you get paid because a rally is likely. Sell calls during overbought spikes (RSI > 70) with a strike 10–20% above the current spot. You are betting the move won't exceed that "buffer" within 30 days.
  • Explosive Math: If you hold 10,000 shares with a $125k basis and sell calls for ~$23/share just 8 times a year, you generate **$1.8 Million in annual income**. That is a **1,440% annual return** on your initial capital. One year of successful boosting can fund decades of lifestyle expenses.
  • Cash Flow Conversion: The end game isn't just "more shares"; it's converting equity into realized, spendable cash without selling your "Golden Goose." In bull markets, premiums are rich; in flat markets, they are steady. You win in both.
IA Insight: The only real danger to the Turbo Booster is "Assignment Risk." If the stock rockets past your strike, the shares are called away. Only use this strategy on spikes confirmed by backtested mean reversion models to ensure you don't lose your seat on the moon mission.
