Methodology & how the math works

For each line: implied value ($B) = revenue ($M) × net margin × P/E ÷ 1,000. The same margin and P/E are applied to both the 2030 and 2035 revenue (a single forward multiple on target-year earnings). Summing all 12 lines gives implied equity value (market cap); dividing by diluted shares gives the implied share price. Implied annual return compounds from today's $417.26 over ~4.5 yrs (2030) and ~9.5 yrs (2035). This is an undiscounted target-year valuation — it does not discount future value back to today, so think of the share price as the price you'd expect at that future date if the assumptions hold.

Pre-revenue/early lines (Robotaxi, Optimus, AI) are valued the same way once they generate revenue and earnings. Because they start near zero, almost all of their value sits in the assumptions you set — they are the swing factor between scenarios.

Read this before trusting any number. Tesla reports only three segments (Automotive, Energy generation & storage, Services & other). The 12-line split here is an analytical construct. The FY2025 revenue for FSD & software, Supercharging, Insurance, Solar, Regulatory credits and Leasing are estimates — Tesla does not disclose them individually — calibrated to sum to the reported $94.8B. All 2030/2035 figures, margins and multiples are illustrative starting assumptions, not consensus forecasts; analyst price targets for TSLA span a very wide range. The default "Balanced" case deliberately values the company on fundamentals and lands below today's price in 2030 — i.e. it shows the current quote already prices in heavy execution of robotaxi/Optimus. Adjust everything to your own view. This is not investment advice.
