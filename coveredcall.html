// MANUAL How to use this tool
What this tool does

This is a decision engine for managing MSTR covered calls using the 10Δ strategy. Each week when you check your position, you enter three things — your current delta, your DTE, and a rough P/L — and it tells you exactly what action to take and how to execute it step by step.

The 5 inputs explained
Position Status Whether you currently have an open short call or not. If you don't, the tool skips straight to opening instructions.
Current Delta (Δ) The delta your short call is showing right now, not when you opened it. Check your broker's options chain. It updates live during market hours.
Current DTE Days to expiration remaining on your short call today. Below 30 DTE is a red flag — the tool will tell you to roll regardless of delta.
Stock Direction A rough read on whether MSTR has been going up, sideways, or down since you opened the position. Used for context in the recommendation.
P/L Slider How much of the premium you've captured so far, as a percentage. At +75% or more, the tool suggests closing early and re-opening rather than waiting for the last few cents.
The 6 outcomes & what triggers them
Hold / On Target Delta is 9–13Δ and DTE is 30+. You're in the sweet spot. Check back next week.
Roll Down Delta dropped below 8Δ because the stock fell. Roll down to the new 10Δ strike and collect extra credit.
Roll Up & Out Delta is 14–22Δ. Stock is creeping up. Roll up to match delta at 40 DTE, pushing the strike further away.
Aggressive Roll Delta is 23–35Δ. Stock is running hard. Roll immediately, accept a smaller credit, and keep extending the DTE.
Emergency / Assignment Delta above 35Δ. Choose: fight to keep shares with an aggressive roll, or accept assignment and execute a buy-write the next morning.
Early Profit P/L slider at 75%+. The theta has decayed enough — close and re-open at 40 DTE to restart the clock.
The golden rules baked into this tool
1Always target 40 DTE when opening or rolling. Never let DTE drop below 30.
2Always use delta to find the strike — never a fixed dollar amount OTM. The 10Δ strike changes every day.
3When rolling, match the current delta at the new expiry. This almost always generates a credit.
4If a roll generates a loss, carry it forward into the new position. Time will work it out.
5Do not sell weeklies. The gamma risk when the stock spikes makes rolling nearly impossible without losing shares.
